Heating & Cooling

Main Room

The split unit is controlled by a thermostat on the windowsill. It handles both heat and AC.

Bathroom

There's a baseboard heater with a knob — turn it as needed.

Bunk & Guest Rooms

The wall thermostat is behind the bunk room door. Use that for both rooms. Please don't touch the baseboard thermostats — they're set where they need to be.

Wood Stove

There's a wood stove in the main room, and using it is one of the best parts of staying here. Firewood is stocked outside. Open the damper before lighting, start with kindling and small pieces, and let it build. Close the damper partway once the fire is established to control the burn. Make sure the fire is fully out before leaving the house or going to bed.

Kitchen

We keep the kitchen stocked with basics — olive oil, salt, pepper, spices, coffee, tea, and a few other staples. Help yourself to anything that's here. If you use something up, no worries at all.

Trash & Recycling

Shutesbury requires yellow town trash bags — you'll find them under the sink. The trash barrel is under the deck. For recycling, please separate paper from plastics and containers. Recycling goes in the bins next to the barrel.

Explore the Valley

Local Recommendations

Our favorite spots for groceries, hikes, food, drinks, and day trips.

Groceries

Leverett Village Co-op 7 min

Small but well-curated. Great for basics, local produce, and a solid deli counter.

Flis Market ~15 min

A local staple in the valley. Good meat counter, everyday essentials.

Green Fields Market 20 min

Full-size natural grocery store in Greenfield — you can get everything here.

Instacart

Delivery works out here if you'd rather not leave the lake.

Hiking

Lake Wyola State Park 5 min

Easy trails right around the lake. Beautiful and accessible.

Ames Pond / Kestrel Land Trust 5 min

A quiet, peaceful walk through conservation land. Great for birding.

Mount Toby 15 min

A rewarding climb with panoramic views from the summit fire tower. Our favorite hike in the area.

Wendell State Forest 10 min

Miles of wooded trails. Feels remote and wild, even though it's right next door.

Places to Visit

Peace Pagoda 10 min

A Buddhist peace pagoda in the woods of Leverett. Serene, surprising, and worth the visit.

Montague Book Mill 15 min

"Books you don't need in a place you can't find." A used bookstore in a converted mill on the river. Get a coffee and browse for an hour.

Bridge of Flowers 40 min

A retired trolley bridge covered in gardens in Shelburne Falls. Genuinely stunning, especially in summer and fall.

Mass MoCA 1 hr+

One of the best contemporary art museums in the country, in North Adams. Worth the drive if you have a full day.

Places to Eat

Dejabrew 6 min

A quirky, beloved coffee shop and cafe in the middle of the Wendell woods. You have to see it to believe it.

North Village Smokehouse 15 min

Solid barbecue in a no-frills setting. Good portions, good vibe.

Watershed 15 min

Farm-to-table done right in Montague. Great for a nicer dinner out.

Wagon Wheel 20 min

Classic roadside restaurant in Gill with huge portions and a loyal following. Cash only.

Places to Drink

Lady Killigrew 15 min

A cozy pub at the Book Mill with great beer, good food, and a patio overlooking the river.

4 Star Farms & Brewery 20 min

A working farm with a taproom in Northfield. Beautiful setting, excellent beer.

Treehouse Brewery 20 min

If you know, you know. Some of the best beer in New England. Plan for a line, but it moves fast.

Element Brewery 10 min

Small, local, and laid-back in Millers Falls. A nice spot to grab a pint without the crowds.

Year-Round

Seasonal Guide

Every season at the lake has its own magic. Here's what we love most.

🏖️

Swim at Hans Bietsch Beach every single day. You're two minutes away — take advantage of it.

🛶

Take the kayaks out in the early morning or late afternoon when the lake is glass.

🥾

Hike Mount Toby before it gets hot and reward yourself with a Treehouse beer run on the way home.

🔥

Spend an evening around the firepit. Marshmallows are probably in the pantry.

😌

Do absolutely nothing on the deck. This is not only allowed, it's encouraged.

🍂

The foliage around the lake is unreal — peak color is usually mid-October. Just sit on the deck and take it in.

🍎

Go apple picking at a local orchard. The valley is full of them.

🗼

Hike Mount Toby for the fire tower views when the leaves are turning. Bring a camera.

🪵

Light the wood stove for the first time and let the cabin fill with that smoky warmth. It changes everything.

🌉

Drive out to the Bridge of Flowers before the season ends — it's magical in autumn light.

🔥

The wood stove is your best friend. Keep it going all day and let the cabin get toasty.

❄️

Hike Wendell State Forest after a snowfall. The quiet in those woods is something else entirely.

🍲

Have a cozy day inside — cook a big meal, play games, read books, watch the snow fall on the lake.

⛷️

Ski Berkshire East — about 45 minutes away. Great for a day trip, solid mountain for all levels.

✨

Bundle up and walk the lake road at night. The stars out here will stop you in your tracks.

🌸

Watch for wildflowers along the trails — they start popping in April and keep going through May.

🦶

Test the lake water with your toes. It's cold, but the brave get bragging rights.

☮️

Visit the Peace Pagoda gardens when everything is blooming. It's a peaceful way to spend a morning.

📚

Browse the Book Mill on a rainy afternoon. Grab a coffee at the cafe and a stack of paperbacks.

🌿

Open every window in the cabin and let the fresh air in. Spring up here smells incredible.
