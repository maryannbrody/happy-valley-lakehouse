**Guest Room**
A queen bed on the first floor — simple, quiet, and comfortable.

**Wood Stove**
There's a wood stove in the main room, and using it is one of the best parts of staying here. Firewood is stocked outside. Open the damper before lighting, start with kindling and small pieces, and let it build. Close the damper partway once the fire is established to control the burn. Make sure the fire is fully out before leaving the house or going to bed.

## On the Water

The lake is the whole point, and we've got you covered.

In the backyard you'll find a **canoe**, **two kayaks**, and a **paddle boat** — all yours to use. Life jackets are in the bin to the right of the deck. Please grab one for everyone heading out.

## Local Recommendations

### Groceries

- **Leverett Village Co-op** — 7 min. Small but well-curated. Great for basics, local produce, and a solid deli counter.
- **Flis Market** — ~15 min. A local staple in the valley. Good meat counter, everyday essentials.
- **Green Fields Market** — 20 min, in Greenfield. Full-size natural grocery store — you can get everything here.
- **Instacart** — Delivery works out here if you'd rather not leave the lake.

### Hiking

- **Lake Wyola State Park** — 5 min. Easy trails right around the lake. Beautiful and accessible.
- **Ames Pond / Kestrel Land Trust** — 5 min. A quiet, peaceful walk through conservation land. Great for birding.
- **Mount Toby** — 15 min. A rewarding climb with panoramic views from the summit fire tower. Our favorite hike in the area.
- **Wendell State Forest** — 10 min. Miles of wooded trails. Feels remote and wild, even though it's right next door.

### Places to Visit

- **Peace Pagoda** — 10 min. A Buddhist peace pagoda in the woods of Leverett. Serene, surprising, and worth the visit.
- **Montague Book Mill** — 15 min. "Books you don't need in a place you can't find." A used bookstore in a converted mill on the river. Get a coffee and browse for an hour.
- **Bridge of Flowers** — 40 min, in Shelburne Falls. A retired trolley bridge covered in gardens. Genuinely stunning, especially in summer and fall.
- **Mass MoCA** — 1 hr+, in North Adams. One of the best contemporary art museums in the country. Worth the drive if you have a full day.

### Places to Eat

- **Dejabrew** — 6 min, in Wendell. A quirky, beloved coffee shop and cafe in the middle of the woods. You have to see it to believe it.
- **North Village Smokehouse** — 15 min. Solid barbecue in a no-frills setting. Good portions, good vibe.
- **Watershed** — 15 min, in Montague. Farm-to-table done right. Great for a nicer dinner out.
- **Wagon Wheel** — 20 min, in Gill. Classic roadside restaurant with huge portions and a loyal following. Cash only.

### Places to Drink

- **Lady Killigrew** — 15 min, at the Book Mill. A cozy pub with great beer, good food, and a patio overlooking the river.
- **4 Star Farms & Brewery** — 20 min, in Northfield. A working farm with a taproom. Beautiful setting, excellent beer.
- **Treehouse Brewery** — 20 min, in Deerfield. If you know, you know. Some of the best beer in New England. Plan for a line, but it moves fast.
- **Element Brewery** — 10 min, in Millers Falls. Small, local, and laid-back. A nice spot to grab a pint without the crowds.

## Seasonal Guide

### Summer
- Swim at Hans Bietsch Beach every single day. You're two minutes away — take advantage of it.
- Take the kayaks out in the early morning or late afternoon when the lake is glass.
- Hike Mount Toby before it gets hot and reward yourself with a Treehouse beer run on the way home.
- Spend an evening around the firepit. Marshmallows are probably in the pantry.
- Do absolutely nothing on the deck. This is not only allowed, it's encouraged.

### Fall
- The foliage around the lake is unreal — peak color is usually mid-October. Just sit on the deck and take it in.
- Go apple picking at a local orchard. The valley is full of them.
- Hike Mount Toby for the fire tower views when the leaves are turning. Bring a camera.
- Light the wood stove for the first time and let the cabin fill with that smoky warmth. It changes everything.
- Drive out to the Bridge of Flowers before the season ends — it's magical in autumn light.

### Winter
- The wood stove is your best friend. Keep it going all day and let the cabin get toasty.
- Hike Wendell State Forest after a snowfall. The quiet in those woods is something else entirely.
- Have a cozy day inside — cook a big meal, play games, read books, watch the snow fall on the lake.
- Ski Berkshire East — about 45 minutes away. Great for a day trip, solid mountain for all levels.
- Bundle up and walk the lake road at night. The stars out here will stop you in your tracks.

### Spring
- Watch for wildflowers along the trails — they start popping in April and keep going through May.
- Test the lake water with your toes. It's cold, but the brave get bragging rights.
- Visit the Peace Pagoda gardens when everything is blooming. It's a peaceful way to spend a morning.
- Browse the Book Mill on a rainy afternoon. Grab a coffee at the cafe and a stack of paperbacks.
- Open every window in the cabin and let the fresh air in. Spring up here smells incredible.
