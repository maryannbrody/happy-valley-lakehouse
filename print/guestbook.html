The Cabin

Our cabin is small but thoughtful — every room has a purpose and a personality.

Main Room

The living room and kitchen share one open space with big windows facing the lake. It's the heart of the house — cook, eat, read, or just stare at the water.

Loft Bedroom

Up the stairs — a queen bed tucked under the roofline. The morning light up here is something special.

Guest Room

A queen bed on the first floor. Simple, quiet, and comfortable.

Bunk Room

Four twin beds. Perfect for kids (or kids at heart). Cozy and everyone loves it.

Bathroom

Recently renovated by us — yes, we DIY'd the whole thing. Towels, soap, and shampoo are all provided.

Heating & Cooling

  • Main Room — The split unit is controlled by a thermostat on the windowsill. Handles both heat and AC.
  • Bathroom — Baseboard heater with a knob. Turn as needed.
  • Bunk & Guest Rooms — Wall thermostat behind the bunk room door controls both rooms. Please don't touch the baseboard thermostats in these rooms.

Wood Stove

Firewood is stocked outside. Open the damper before lighting, start with kindling and small pieces, and let it build. Close the damper partway once the fire is established to control the burn. Make sure the fire is fully out before leaving or going to bed.

Kitchen

Stocked with basics — olive oil, salt, pepper, spices, coffee, tea, and more. Help yourself to anything that's here. If you use something up, no worries.

Trash & Recycling

Shutesbury requires yellow town trash bags — you'll find them under the sink. The barrel is under the deck. Separate paper from plastics for recycling; bins are next to the barrel.

On the Water

In the backyard you'll find a canoe, two kayaks, and a paddle boat — all yours to use. Life jackets are in the bin to the right of the deck.

Please return boats to the yard before you leave. Guests are responsible for their own safety on the water.

Swimming

  • Hans Bietsch Beach 2-min walk Our go-to spot. Head right at the bottom of the driveway. Small, quiet, and perfect.
  • Lake Wyola State Beach ½ mile Sandy beach with parking and lifeguards in summer. Great if you want more space.

Beach towels are in the upstairs dresser — grab as many as you need.

Local Recommendations

Groceries

  • Leverett Village Co-op 7 min Small but well-curated. Great basics, local produce, solid deli.
  • Flis Market ~15 min Local staple. Good meat counter, everyday essentials.
  • Green Fields Market 20 min Full-size natural grocery in Greenfield. Has everything.
  • Instacart Delivery works out here if you'd rather stay at the lake.

Hiking

  • Lake Wyola State Park 5 min Easy trails right around the lake.
  • Ames Pond / Kestrel Land Trust 5 min Quiet conservation land. Great for birding.
  • Mount Toby 15 min Panoramic views from the fire tower. Our favorite hike.
  • Wendell State Forest 10 min Miles of wooded trails. Feels remote and wild.

Places to Visit

  • Montague Book Mill 15 min "Books you don't need in a place you can't find."
  • Bridge of Flowers 40 min Garden-covered bridge in Shelburne Falls. Stunning.

Places to Eat

Seasonal Guide

Every season at the lake has its own magic. Here's what we love most.

☀ Summer
  • Swim at Hans Bietsch Beach every single day — you're two minutes away.
  • Kayak in the early morning or late afternoon when the lake is glass.
  • Hike Mount Toby before it gets hot, then reward yourself with a Treehouse beer run.
  • Spend an evening around the firepit. Marshmallows are probably in the pantry.
  • Do absolutely nothing on the deck. This is encouraged.
🍂 Fall
  • The foliage around the lake is unreal — peak color is usually mid-October.
  • Go apple picking at a local orchard. The valley is full of them.
  • Hike Mount Toby for fire tower views when the leaves are turning.
  • Light the wood stove for the first time. It changes everything.
  • Drive out to the Bridge of Flowers — it's magical in autumn light.
❄ Winter
  • The wood stove is your best friend. Keep it going all day.
  • Hike Wendell State Forest after a snowfall. The quiet is something else.
  • Have a cozy day in — cook a big meal, play games, watch the snow fall.
  • Ski Berkshire East, about 45 minutes away. Great day trip.
  • Bundle up and walk the lake road at night. The stars will stop you.
🌸 Spring
  • Watch for wildflowers along the trails — April through May.
  • Test the lake water with your toes. It's cold, but the brave get bragging rights.
  • Visit the Peace Pagoda gardens when everything is blooming.
  • Browse the Book Mill on a rainy afternoon. Grab a coffee and a stack of paperbacks.
  • Open every window and let the fresh air in. Spring up here smells incredible.
